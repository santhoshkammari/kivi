### 2.1 OpenAI Re-Organizes: Greg Brockman Takes Product Control

**Source:** WIRED — Maxwell Zeff, May 15, 2026

OpenAI cofounder and president **Greg Brockman** now officially leads the company's **product strategy**, in addition to his AI infrastructure work. This role was previously interim while Fidji Simo (CEO of AGI deployment) was on medical leave.

#### Product Consolidation

OpenAI is merging three key products into one core product team:

| Product | Description | Status |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| **ChatGPT** | Consumer chatbot | Merging into unified product |
| **Codex** | AI coding agent | Merging into unified product |
| **Developer API** | API for developers | Merging into unified product |

#### Key Quote

> *"We're consolidating our product efforts to execute with maximum focus toward the agentic future, to win across both consumer and enterprise."*
> — **Greg Brockman**, WIRED

#### Executive Role Changes

| Executive | Previous Role | New Role |
|-----------|--------------|----------|
| **Thibault Sottiaux** | Head of Codex | Leads core product & platform teams; overseeing "super app" combining Codex, ChatGPT, and Atlas browser |
| **Nick Turley** | Head of ChatGPT | Leads enterprise products; no longer working on consumer products |
| **Ashley Alexander** | Head of health products | Takes over consumer product unit |
| **Fidji Simo** | CEO of AGI deployment | Remains on medical leave |

#### Context

- **Competitive pressure:** Anthropic in coding, Google in consumer chatbots
- **IPO plans:** Leadership wants to simplify products ahead of potential IPO later in 2026
- **Recent departures:** Kevin Weil (AI workspace for scientists), Bill Peebles (head of Sora), Srinivas Narayanan (CTO of enterprise applications)

### 2.2 ChatGPT Gets Bank Account Access via Plaid

**Source:** The Verge — Jess Weatherbed, May 15, 2026

OpenAI announced a new feature letting users securely connect ChatGPT to their bank accounts via **Plaid**, the bank-to-app bridging platform used by 12,000 financial institutions including Schwab, Fidelity, Chase, and Capital One.

#### Financial Planning Features

ChatGPT can:
- View a **dashboard** detailing spending history and active subscriptions
- Help with major financial decisions (buying a house, signing up for credit cards)
- **Flag changes in spending habits**
- Connect to both **bank accounts and investment platforms**

#### What ChatGPT Can See

| Data Type | Accessible? |
|-----------|-------------|
| Balances | ✅ Yes |
| Transactions | ✅ Yes |
| Stock portfolio | ✅ Yes |
| Liabilities (mortgage, credit card debt) | ✅ Yes |
| Full account numbers | ❌ No |
| Ability to make changes | ❌ No |

#### Availability

| Tier | Access | Price |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Pro** | Available now (US only) | $200/month |
| **Plus** | Coming next | $20/month |
| **Free** | Eventual goal | Free |

#### Security & Privacy

- Users can **disconnect bank accounts** at any time (up to 30 days to delete data)
- Users can view and delete **"financial memories"** saved by the chatbot
- Control over whether data trains AI models (opt-in option)
- OpenAI **doesn't specify** what the company will do with financial information outside of AI training

#### Key Quote

> *"But it can see your balances, transactions, stock portfolio, and liabilities like a mortgage and credit card debt. And OpenAI — a company that eventually needs to turn a profit — doesn't specify what the company itself will do with all that financial information outside of AI training, or if there are any additional protections in place to protect users' financial data from a system hack. What could possibly go wrong?"*
> — **Jess Weatherbed**, The Verge

### 2.5 OpenAI Launches Codex in ChatGPT Mobile

**Source:** eWeek — Liz Ticong, May 15, 2026

OpenAI brings **Codex** to the **ChatGPT mobile app**, letting users steer coding-agent work from their phones as weekly use tops **4 million users**.

#### Key Details

- Codex accessible through the sidebar in ChatGPT
- Users can assign new coding tasks by typing a prompt and clicking "Code"
- Each task processed independently in a separate, isolated environment preloaded with the user's codebase
- One of OpenAI's "fastest-growing products of all time"

### 2.6 OpenAI Shuts Down Sora — Disney Deal Cancelled

**Source:** Variety — Cynthia Littleton (Daily Variety podcast), March 26, 2026

OpenAI **abruptly pulled the plug** on its generative AI video creation model, **Sora**. Disney's character licensing deal and **$1 billion investment** in OpenAI have been called off.

#### Reasons for Shutdown

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Compute intensity** | Generative AI for video is "extremely compute intensive" |
| **Financial pressures** | OpenAI preparing for IPO in 2026 — needs to show profitability |
| **No clear monetization** | Sora was a "very high cost product" with unresolved rights issues |
| **Strategic refocus** | Focus shifted to core AI apps and products amid intense competition |

#### Key Quote

> *"The fact that OpenAI basically unilaterally shut this down doesn't mean Disney doesn't want to do this. It just means that this version of it didn't pan out. I think the threat is still there."*
> — **Gene Maddaus**, Variety (Daily Variety podcast)

#### Impact

| Deal | Status |
|------|--------|
| Disney character licensing (200+ Marvel, Pixar, Star Wars characters) | ❌ Cancelled |
| Disney $1 billion investment in OpenAI | ❌ Cancelled |
| Sora consumer app | ❌ Shut down |
| Internal "world simulation" research | ✅ Continues (for robotics) |
